## Cross-functional collaboration:
Actively contributes across different areas of the project and supports teammates with complementary skills to ensure the team can deliver complete, high-quality increments each sprint.

## Self-organization and accountability:
Takes ownership of tasks, manages workload effectively, and supports the team in planning and prioritizing work without needing constant direction.

## Empowerment and autonomy:
Exercises sound judgment in decision-making, aligns with team goals, and takes initiative to remove blockers and drive progress independently.

## Strong communication and collaboration:
Maintains open, respectful, and transparent communication within the team and with stakeholders, ensuring clarity and alignment throughout the sprint.

## Commitment to continuous improvement:
Actively participates in retrospectives, provides constructive feedback, and helps implement process improvements that enhance team velocity and quality.

## Customer and value focus:
Keeps the end user in mind when making technical and design decisions, ensuring that deliverables align with business priorities and add measurable value.

## Quality-driven delivery:
Upholds a clear Definition of Done, promotes clean code and testing practices, and ensures that every deliverable meets or exceeds agreed standards.

## Adaptability and responsiveness:
Adjusts quickly to changing priorities and requirements while maintaining a positive attitude and keeping the team focused on outcomes.

## Shared ownership and trust:
Fosters a supportive environment where teammates feel safe to share ideas, raise concerns, and innovate without fear of blame.

## Data-informed improvement:
Uses metrics like velocity, code quality trends, and deployment feedback to guide discussions and improve both team performance and delivery consistency.
