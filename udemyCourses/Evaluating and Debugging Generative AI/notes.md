## Frechet Inception Distance (FID)

- Metric for determining how well a particular GAN model generates realistic and drivers images; measures how similar the generated images are to the real ones.
- Compare FID scores to determine diference in performance.
- THe lower the number, the closer the images are to the real data distribution.

### FID Score

- Computed by compring values (mean and covariance of activations) of real and generated images.
- Zero is a perfect score.
- The lower the FID score, the better th model's performance when generating fake images.

---

## Bilingual Evaluation Understudy (BLEU)

- Metric used to evaluate text models.
- For example, you are using a Model to translate a poem from one language to another.

### BLEU Score

- Determines how well machine-translated text matches one or more human reference translations.
- Metric can be between zero and one.
    -   One = Perfect Match
    -   Zero = no overlap between the machine and human reference translations.

## Common Problems
- Mode collapse
- Vanishing gradiants

Additional information on common problems can be found in [here](./commonIssues.md)

### Mode Collapse

- typicall occurs on GANs
    -Generator generates data that fools the discriminator.
    -Discriminator generates divers data samples.


### Vanishing Gradiants

- The vanishing gradient problem is a common issue in training deep neural networks, especially those with many layers or using certain activation functions like sigmoid or tanh.

💡 Analogy: Think of passing a message through 50 people where each person whispers 10% of what they heard. By the end, the original message is almost gone—that’s what happens to the gradient in vanishing gradients.

#### Solutions / Workarounds

Use ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit) or its variants (Leaky ReLU, GELU) instead of sigmoid/tanh.

Batch Normalization to stabilize activations.

Proper weight initialization (like He or Xavier initialization).

Residual connections (ResNets) that allow gradients to flow directly across layers.

Gradient clipping (mostly for RNNs) to prevent exploding gradients but can also help with vanishing issues in practice.

# Section 4: Discussing Ethics and Ensuring Fairness

- Imagine a generative model trained to create professional headshots. If the training data predominantly consists of images of one ethnic group, the model may struggle to generate diverse images, reinforcing stereotypes and excluding other groups.
- Language models can exhibit bias by producing content that reflects societal prejudices. For example, if a model generates job descriptions or essays that favor one gender over another, it can perpetuate discrimination.
- Generative models can create realistic fake images or videos, known as deep fakes? These can be used maliciously to create false representations of people, leading to privacy violations and potential harm.
- Sometimes generative models can reveal information from their training data, inadvertently exposing sensitive details.
- You can protect privacy by anonymizing data before using it to train models. This can involve removing or masking personally identifiable information.
- Make sure to establish and adhere to strict data handling policies to safeguard personal information and ensure compliance with data protection regulations.

## Tools and Techniques to Detect BIAS in your models.

There are tools and techniques to detect bias in your models.

- Make sure to establish and adhere to strict data handling policies to safeguard personal informatin and ensure compliance with data protection regulations.
- As GenAI developers and users, we must be accountable for the systems we create and deploy.
- It's up to us to ensure that AI systems are transparent and their decision-making processes explainable.
- This will help users and stakeholders understand how and why certain outputs are generated.
- Regular audits can ensure compliance with ethical standards and help identify areas for improvement. 

## Types Of BIAS

1. Historical bias: Historical bias comes from historical inequalities and prejudices in the training data that reflects society. 
2. Representation bias: Representation bias happens when certain groups are underrepresented in the training data. 
3. Measurement Bias : Inaccuracies in data collection process

## Mitigate Bias

- Examine training data
- Use fairness metrics
- Review model outputs

### Some examples:

#### Ethical Guidelines on Hiring Process

1. What ethical conrerns could arise from using AI in hiring?
2. What can we do to ensure fairness and transparency in the hiring process?

There are several guidelines we can put in place. 
- Bias audits: Regularly audit AI systems for biases and take corrective action. 
- Transparency: Clearly communicate how AI is used in the hiring process to applicants.
- Fairness: Ensure the training data is diverse and representative of all demographic groups.
- Human oversight: Implement human oversight to review AI decisions and make the final hiring decisions. 

#### Ethical Guidelines on Healthcare

1. What are the ethical implications of using AI in healthcare?
2. How can we ensure the safety and accuracy of AI-driven medical diagnosis?

AI is often used to diagnose patients and recommend treatments. However, there are concerns about the AI's accuracy and the potential for misdiagnoses.

Below are some ethical guidelines:

- Accuracy Verification:  Continuously verify and validate the accuracy of AI systems in a medical diagnosis.
- Transparency: Explain AI-driven diagnoses and treatment recommendations clearly to patients and healthcare providers.
- Patient consent: Obtain informed consent from patients before using AI-driven medical tools.
- Accountability: Establish clear accountability mechanisms for errors or a misdiagnosis made by AI systems.
